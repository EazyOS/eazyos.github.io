> Download & Start The Setup

To install EazyOS, you need to download the setup which can be accessed on the official EazyOS website or downloaded directly from this link.

Attention Firefox Users!

Some Firefox users might experience difficulty downloading the setup file directly from our website.

To run setup, open your File Explorer, navigate to your Downloads folder, and double-click "setup3.1.1.bat".

> Going Through The Installer

Once you boot up the installer, you should see a terminal interface similar to the image below.

To continue setting up EazyOS, press the Enter key to install EazyOS to the default path (recommended), or enter your own custom path to install (advanced).

Next, select "Fresh Install (removes everything and reinstalls)" by pressing the 1 key on your keyboard to clean up any potential leftover EazyOS installations.

Alternatively, you can select "Upgrade EazyOS (keeps your user data)" by pressing 2 on your keyboard to upgrade your version while keeping your files intact.

If you choose a fresh installation, setup will prompt you for a username and password. Enter your desired login credentials for EazyOS.

Next, wait for EazyOS to download (this might take a while depending on your internet speed & connection).

After the first half of the process is finished, the second half of setup will install other required backend applications.

> Logging into EazyOS

Once the installer is finished, you should be greeted by the window pictured below.

When EazyOS prompts you to sign in, type the custom username and password you configured during setup.

> Customizing EazyOS

To customize your environment, open up the Control Panel located on the desktop background after logging in.

To change the background color, click on "Display". Under "Desktop Background", click the "Choose" button and select your preferred color accent.

To change the background image, click on "Display". Under the "Wallpaper" section, select a default background choice or click on the "Browse..." button, choose your file, and click "Set Wallpaper".

To modify the titlebar color, click on "Display". Under the "Window Title Bar" section, click on the "Choose..." button and select a color theme. Modifying your system taskbar color follows a nearly identical process.

> Changing Your Username and Password

Open the system Start Menu and select the "Add Username and Password" utility.

From there, simply input your new username and password configurations.

Always Remember To...

  • Not ignore the "EazyOS Out of Support" alert message.
